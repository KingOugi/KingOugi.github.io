Banana Bread Pancakes

Fluffy, cozy, and full of banana bread flavor, these pancakes are the perfect weekend treat. The edges get golden and buttery while the centers stay soft and full of melty chocolate chips. Stack them high, drizzle with maple syrup, and watch them disappear in seconds.

Prep Time: 10 minutes     Cook Time: 15 minutes

Total Time: 25 minutes     Yields: 6 pancakes



Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted (plus more for pan)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup cold buttermilk
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/4 cup chocolate chips

For Serving

  • Maple syrup


Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas until mostly smooth with a few small chunks left.
  2. Whisk in the egg, melted butter, vanilla extract, and buttermilk until combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and gently fold until just combined. Do not overmix.
  5. Heat a nonstick pan or griddle over medium heat and add butter to melt.
  6. Scoop about 1/2 cup of batter for each pancake. Sprinkle walnuts and chocolate chips on top before flipping.
  7. Cook until bubbles form and edges look set, then flip and cook until golden brown.
  8. Transfer to a plate and repeat with remaining batter, adding more butter as needed.
  9. Serve warm with butter, maple syrup, and extra chocolate chips.


Tips & Notes

Can I use oil instead of butter?
You can, but the pancakes won’t be as flavorful. Butter browns as it cooks, giving a richer taste.

How can I make them taste more like banana bread?
Add thin banana slices on top of the batter before flipping. They caramelize beautifully.

What if I don’t have buttermilk?
Combine 1 cup of milk with 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar and let it sit for 5 minutes.

Can I save leftovers?
Yes! Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days or freeze with parchment paper between pancakes.
